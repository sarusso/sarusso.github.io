About me


I hold a MSc in computational physics with a thesis on Big Data at CERN and a BSc in Computer Science with a thesis on High Performance Computing at SISSA (Trieste, Italy).

I am currently working on data-intensive analysis platforms within the European "ESCAPE" project, at INAF, with a particular focus on reproducibility.

Previously, I co-founded a startup working on infrastructural and environmental monitoring, I have been a cohort memebr at Entrepreneur First London (Europeâ€™s best deep tech accelerator), the head of data at an energy intelligence startup and a CERN fellow.

My side activities include lecturing on Big Data and software development (University of Trieste, SISSA, ICTP), developing some open source projects in the time series and IoT space, and consulting at the intersection of physics and computer science (EUMETSAT, Sky UK, eXact Lab, Idrostudi, and more).

Follow me on Twitter , GitHub or connect with me on LinkedIn !

Projects

Timeseria

Timeseria is a time series processing library. It provides modules for data cleaning, resampling and reconstrution, as well as forecasting and anomaly detection models. [GitHub]

Pythings

Pythings is a quick and easy way of coding Internet of Things devices, directly from a web browser, in Python. It shines on micro-controllers, but if works nicely also on Linux boards [pythings.io] [GitHub]
